Getting Started

Introduction

Cadence is an automated recurring billing and subscription lifecycle engine built specifically on top of Nomba's online payment APIs.

By sitting as an orchestration layer between your software application and Nomba's payment primitives, Cadence automatically handles tokenized card authorizations, billing periods, automatic retries (dunning logic), and webhook states on your behalf.

¡
Looking for merchant controls? Log in to the Merchant Portal to manage your active plans, generate developer tokens, and monitor subscribers.

Quickstart

Getting integration up and running takes less than 5 minutes. Follow these three core steps:

1

Register Merchant & Generate API Key

First, create your merchant account at /api/auth/register (you must provide your nomba_client_id, nomba_client_secret, and nomba_account_id). Then go to your merchant dashboard to generate a Developer API Key (e.g. cd_live_...). Include it in the HTTP headers of your server-side requests.

2

Define a Billing Plan

Define a subscription package (such as a monthly charge of ₦2,000) using the dashboard UI or by hitting the POST /api/plans endpoint.

3

Enroll a Subscriber

When a customer checks out, call the subscriptions endpoint to receive a secure Nomba checkout URL. Redirect your customer to this link to capture and authorize their card.

Subscription Lifecycle

A subscription transitions dynamically between six core lifecycle states depending on payment outcomes, trials, and active cancellations:

trialing

Customer has signed up with a free trial period. No charges have been made. Access is active.

active

Payment is current and subscription is valid for the current billing cycle. Full access granted.

past_due

A renewal invoice payment failed. The engine enters dunning state and continues retrying. Grace access active.

suspended

All dunning retry attempts have been exhausted (3 retries). User access is frozen until card update.

cancelled

The subscription was cancelled either immediately by the merchant or at period end by the subscriber. Terminal state.

expired

The underlying billing plan was permanently deactivated or ended. Terminal state.

Dunning & Automatic Retries

When a renewal payment fails, the subscription is moved to the past_due state and the dunning scheduler automatically schedules retries over a **11-day grace period** before suspension:

Attempt Interval Failure Action Success Action
Initial renewal Renewal Date Transition to past_due, retry 1 scheduled Stay active, advance period
Retry 1 1 day later Stay past_due, retry 2 scheduled Transition to active, clear retries
Retry 2 3 days after Retry 1 Stay past_due, retry 3 scheduled Transition to active, clear retries
Retry 3 7 days after Retry 2 Transition to suspended, stop retries Transition to active, clear retries

Pre-flight Dual-Write Protection

In developing regions, network connection failures or application crashes immediately after charging a user card but prior to database commit can lead to dangerous double-charging.

Cadence features **Pre-flight Verification Protection**. Before executing any automatic renewal card charge:

  • The engine scans for any existing pending payments initiated in the current cycle.
  • If found, Cadence executes a **pre-flight requery call** to Nomba's verify_transaction API to verify the downstream status.
  • If Nomba confirms that the payment succeeded, Cadence automatically **bypasses the new charge**, consumes the token key, advances the subscription's billing period, and writes it to the database, ensuring zero double-charging.
